How the development actually worked

The workflow.

1
Describe it, don't spec it
No design docs. No PRDs. A short message to Claude Code: "I want a live World Cup tracker with a clean card layout, match stakes, and push notifications." It asked one or two clarifying questions — then started building.
2
Review and redirect in real time
Claude runs the app in a browser, takes screenshots, reports what's working. When something looks wrong, you say "the scores are in the wrong order" or "that notification is too aggressive" — it fixes and pushes.
3
Production on day one
Vercel deploys from GitHub on every push. The app was live — accessible to real users — while it was still being built. Users found bugs before we did. We shipped fixes mid-session.
4
Phone as the product manager
Once live, the laptop closed. Day-to-day changes came from the Claude.ai app on iPhone. "Change the font size on the header" or "the recap for June 24 has a factual error" — done before the next ad break.
5
Gemini picks up what Claude built
The AI content side — player verdicts, advanced stats modelling, the pundit persona — was handed to Gemini. Different model, same codebase, different skill set. Complementary, not competing.
Key Shares

What we learnt.

Things we'd do differently — and things we'd never go back on.

01 / Speed
Ship something ugly, fast
The first version looked rough. It didn't matter. Real users on real devices uncovered more issues in 24 hours than any internal review could in a week. Start ugly, ship fast, iterate in public.
02 / Prompting
Direction beats specification
The best results came from explaining the intent, not the implementation. "Make it feel like a premium app" outperformed "set border-radius to 12px." The AI infers; you steer.
03 / Trust
Know when to verify
AI gets the 95% right automatically. The 5% — AI saying a team was eliminated when they had a slim best-third route — required human judgement. Never hand over edge cases without a review step.
04 / Models
Pick the right AI for the job
Claude Code for engineering: file operations, debugging, complex multi-step builds. Gemini for creative content: pundit personas, player verdicts, entertainment copy. They have different strengths.
05 / Scale
No ops overhead is a superpower
Zero servers to manage. GitHub Actions for the pipeline. Vercel for hosting. Upstash for Redis. Every service is fully managed — all focus went into the product, none into infrastructure.
06 / Team
One person. Three AIs. A full product.
This would have taken a team of four — frontend, backend, data engineering, AI/ML — three months. Two people, three AI tools, days. The economics of building have permanently shifted.
